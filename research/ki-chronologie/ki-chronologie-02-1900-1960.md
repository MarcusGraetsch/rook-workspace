# 02 - 1900–1960 (Kybernetik, Turing, Frühe Computer)

> Teil der KI-Chronologie-Sammlung

### Alexander Belyaev: Ariel (1923) / The Ruler of the World (1926)
- Ariel: A man with electromagnetic flight technology — anti-gravity as social liberation
- The Ruler of the World: Mind control technology and global power
- **Technology as power — the Soviet dream of science conquering nature**
- Quelle: SF Encyclopedia, Wikipedia

### Karel Čapek: R.U.R. (1920)
- "Rossum's Universal Robots"
- Das Wort "Robot" wurde hier geboren (tschechisch: "robota" = Fronarbeit)
- Roboter rebellieren gegen ihre menschlichen Herren
- **Weltweiter Erfolg — die Angst vor der Maschine wird sprachlich**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, History.com, ResearchGate

### Yevgeny Zamyatin: We (1924)
- Sowjetische Dystopie, geschrieben 1920-1921
- Die Gesellschaft wird durch die "Benevolent Machine" kontrolliert
- Jeder Mensch ist nur eine Nummer, jeder Aspekt des Lebens wird algorithmisch reguliert
- **Einfluss auf Orwell, Huxley, Bradbury — die Mutter aller modernen Dystopien**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, Damien Walter, Clarkesworld Magazine

### Karel Čapek: *R.U.R.* (1920) — *The Makropulos Affair* (1922) — *Krakatit* (1924)
- Čapek (1890–1938), Czech playwright and novelist, coined the word "robot"
- *R.U.R.*: Rossum's Universal Robots — the birth of the word "robot" (Czech: *robota* = forced labor)
- *The Makropulos Affair*: immortality through a 300-year elixir — the ethical cost of eternal life
- *Krakatit*: an atomic phantasy about a weapon of absolute destructive power
- **Čapek's broader vision: robots, immortality, and atomic destruction**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, SF Encyclopedia, ISFDB, Encyclopedia.com

### Olaf Stapledon: *Last and First Men* (1930) — *Star Maker* (1937) — *Odd John* (1935)
- British philosopher and novelist (1886–1950), hugely influential on later SF
- *Last and First Men*: future history of 18 human species over 2 billion years, including telepathic and genetically engineered descendants
- *Star Maker*: a cosmic voyage through the history of the universe, encountering countless civilizations and their technological fates
- *Odd John*: a superhuman mutant with intelligence beyond normal humans, exploring the ethics of superior beings
- **The philosopher of cosmic scale: humanity's technological future across deep time**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, SF Encyclopedia, Hackwriters, Jeroenthoughts

### Arthur C. Clarke: *Childhood's End* (1953) — *The City and the Stars* (1956) — *The Sentinel* (1951)
- *Childhood's End*: the Overmind — a transcendent cosmic intelligence that absorbs humanity's children
- *The City and the Stars*: Diaspar — a computer-managed city where humans live for 1000 years, recreated by the Central Computer
- *The Sentinel* (1951): the short story that became the basis for *2001: A Space Odyssey*
- **Clarke's cosmic visions: transcendence through technology and the computer-managed utopia**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, EBSCO, Reactor Mag, Excelsior Forever

### Isaac Asimov: *Foundation* (1951) — *The Caves of Steel* (1954) — *The Naked Sun* (1957) — *The Bicentennial Man* (1976)
- *Foundation*: psychohistory — a mathematical science that predicts the future of human masses
- Hari Seldon predicts the fall of the Galactic Empire and engineers a 1000-year recovery
- *The Caves of Steel*: robots integrated into human society, detective Elijah Baley and robot Daneel Olivaw
- *The Naked Sun*: a society where humans have almost no physical contact, served by robots
- *The Bicentennial Man*: Andrew Martin, a robot, fights for 200 years to be recognized as human
- **Asimov's robot-human tapestry: the laws of robotics and the mathematics of history**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, Clarkesworld, Amazon, Goodreads

### Philip K. Dick: *Second Variety* (1953) — *The Minority Report* (1956) — *Ubik* (1969) — *Flow My Tears, the Policeman Said* (1974)
- *Second Variety*: autonomous killing machines ("claws") evolve beyond human control during a nuclear war
- *The Minority Report*: Precrime — a system that uses precognitive mutants to arrest people before they commit crimes
- *Ubik*: a reality-dissolving substance in a world where reality itself is manipulated by technology
- *Flow My Tears*: a police state where identity is fluid and technology controls who exists
- **Dick's paranoid universe: machines that kill, predict, and dissolve reality**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, SF Encyclopedia, Denver SF Book Club, Apple TV


## 3. Die Film-Geschichte (1924–2026)

### Metropolis (1927) — Fritz Lang
- Erster KI-Film der Geschichte
- Robot Maria: gefährlich, weil sie ein vertrauenswürdiges menschliches Gesicht trägt
- Brigitte Helm als "Maschinenmensch" — eine der ersten Roboterdarstellungen
- **Set in 2026 — wir leben jetzt in der Zukunft des Films**
- Maria wurde in die Robot Hall of Fame aufgenommen
- Originale Requisiten im Sony Centre Berlin ausgestellt
- Quelle: Wikipedia, IMDb, Robot Hall of Fame, YouTube "Metropolis 1927"

### Godzilla (1954) — Ishirō Honda
- Nuclear technology run amok — the original "technology destroys humanity"
- Not AI per se, but the template for "uncontrolled technology as monster"
- **The Japanese techno-trauma narrative**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, IMDb

### Desk Set (1957)
- EMERAC / "Emmy" — Computer in einer Bibliothek
- Spencer Tracy & Katharine Hepburn, Computer ersetzt Menschen?
- **Frühe Kritik an Computerisierung des Arbeitsplatzes**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, IMDb

### The Invisible Boy (1957)
- Robby the Robot erneut, diesmal als freundlicher Begleiter
- **Wiederauferstehung von Robby als Sympathieträger**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, IMDb

### Blade Runner (1982) — Ridley Scott
- Replicants: bioengineert, nicht berechnet
- Voigt-Kampff Test: Turing-Test-Analogon
- **Frage: Was macht uns menschlich?**

### The Jetsons (1962) — Hanna-Barbera
- Rosie the Robot Maid — hauswirtschaftliche Roboter-Hilfe
- Futuristische Vision der 1960er Jahre
- **Der Roboter als Dienstmädchen — Alltags-KI**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, IMDb


## 6. Die Musik (1950er–heute)

### Iannis Xenakis: Algorithmische Komposition (1950er–1960er)
- Griechisch-französischer Komponist, Architekt
- "Metastasis" (1953) — mathematische Modelle für Musik
- Stochastic music — Wahrscheinlichkeitsalgorithmen
- **Die Brücke zwischen Mathematik und Kunst**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, Computer Music Journal

### Daphne Oram: Oramics (1950s–1960s)
- British composer and electronic music pioneer (1925–2003)
- Co-founder of the BBC Radiophonic Workshop in 1958
- Created the Oramics Machine: a "drawn-sound" synthesizer that converted hand-drawn graphics into electronic sounds
- The machine used 35mm film strips with painted shapes to generate sound waves
- **The first graphic synthesizer: drawing music as visual art**
- Quelle: BBC Culture, GerenM, Zynthian Discourse, Instagram

## 8. Die akademische und theoretische Grundlage (Pre-1950s → 2020s)

### Ludwig Wittgenstein — Key Works
- *Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus* (1921): "Whereof one cannot speak, thereof one must be silent."
- *Philosophical Investigations* (1953): language as use, meaning as practice
- **Die Grenzen der Sprache sind die Grenzen der KI**
- Quelle: Standardliteratur, Wikipedia

### Steven Pinker: "The Language Instinct" (1994)
- Canadian-American psychologist (born 1954)
- Language as an innate human capacity, not learned behavior
- The mind as a system of specialized modules, each evolved for a specific purpose
- **The language instinct: the mind as a Swiss Army knife of evolved modules**
- Quelle: Standardliteratur, Wikipedia

### Douglas Hofstadter: "I Am a Strange Loop" (2007)
- The self as a "strange loop" — a feedback pattern in the brain
- Consciousness as an emergent property of self-referential systems
- **The self as strange loop: the pattern that thinks it is a person**
- Quelle: Standardliteratur, Wikipedia

### Macy Conferences (1946–1953)
- Interdisziplinäre Konferenzen zu Cybernetics und Feedback-Systemen
- Teilnehmer: Wiener, von Neumann, Bateson, Mead, McCulloch
- **Die Geburtsstunde der Interdisziplinarität, die KI hervorbrachte**
- Quelle: JSTOR, Wikipedia, Kate Vass Gallery

### John von Neumann & Oskar Morgenstern: Game Theory (1944)
- "Theory of Games and Economic Behavior"
- Mathematische Grundlagen für strategisches Verhalten, später zentral für Multi-Agent-Systeme
- **Die mathematische Sprache der KI-Interaktion**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, Princeton

### Vannevar Bush: "As We May Think" (1945)
- The Atlantic Monthly — visionärer Essay über Computer als menschliche Assistenten
- Memex — Konzept für hypertextbasiertes Wissensmanagement
- **Die Vision, die das Internet und KI vorwegahm**
- Quelle: The Atlantic, Wikipedia

### Alan Turing: "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" (1950)
- Turing Test als Maßstab für maschinelle Intelligenz
- "Can machines think?" — die grundlegende Frage
- **Die philosophische Geburtsstunde der KI**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy

### Arthur Samuel: Checkers-Playing Program (1959)
- IBM 701, erste Machine-Learning-Anwendung
- Das Programm lernte durch Selbstspiel und verbesserte sich
- **Samuel prägte den Begriff "Machine Learning"**
- Quelle: IBM, MIT, ResearchGate

### Logic Theorist (1955–1956) — Newell, Simon & Shaw
- Erstes AI-Programm, bewies mathematische Theoreme
- Demonstrierte symbolisches Schließen
- **Der erste Beweis, dass Computer "denken" können**
- Quelle: Carnegie Mellon, AIWS, History of Information

### General Problem Solver (GPS) (1957–1959) — Newell & Simon
- Erweiterung des Logic Theorist, allgemeines Problemlösen
- Means-ends analysis — zentrale Algorithmus-Struktur
- **Die universelle Problemlösungs-Maschine**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, AIWS, Cow-Shed

### Frank Rosenblatt: Perceptron (1958)
- Erstes künstliches neuronales Netzwerk mit Lernfähigkeit
- Cornell Aeronautical Laboratory, hardware-basiert
- **Die Geburtsstunde des Deep Learning**
- Quelle: Cornell, Stanford, S-PRO

### Ross Ashby: Design for a Brain (1952) / An Introduction to Cybernetics (1956)
- The Homeostat (1949): first "thinking machine" that self-regulated to equilibrium
- "Requisite Variety" — a system must match the complexity of its environment
- **The first adaptive machine: cybernetics as self-regulation**
- Quelle: Ashby.info, Medium, York.ac.uk

### Grey Walter: The Living Brain (1953) / Elmer & Elsie (1948–1949)
- Robotic "tortoises" — Elmer and Elsie (ELectro MEchanical Robots, Light-Sensitive)
- First autonomous robots that could navigate, learn, and self-recognize in mirrors
- **The first biological robots: behavior-based AI before the term existed**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, Cybernetic Zoo, ResearchGate, CSULB

### Dartmouth Conference (1956)
- John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, Claude Shannon, Nathaniel Rochester
- The proposal: "We propose that a 2 month, 10 man study of artificial intelligence be carried out"
- **The term "Artificial Intelligence" was coined here — the official birth of AI**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, LivingInternet, Stanford (jmc.stanford.edu), Council of Science

### Bertie the Brain (1950)
- Einer der ersten Computer, die Tic-Tac-Toe spielten
- 4 Meter hoch, Vakuumröhren-basiert
- **Der erste Spielcomputer**
- Quelle: Facebook/CTY Johns Hopkins, Wikipedia

### Samuel Butler: "Darwin among the Machines" (1863)
- Maschinen entwickeln sich wie Organismen
- Könnten Bewusstsein entwickeln und Menschen ersetzen
- **Die Evolutionäre Vision der KI**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, McCorduck

### Alan Turing: Turing Test (1950)
- "Can machines think?" — operationalisiert durch Imitation Game
- Maßstab für künstliche Intelligenz
- **Die operationalisierte Definition von KI**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy
