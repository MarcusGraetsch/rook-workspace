### Joseph Haydn: Musical Dice Game (1793)
- Austrian composer (1732–1809) created a dice-based composition system
- "Gioco filarmonico" or "Philharmonic Joke" — a musical game for creating minuets
- 16 pre-composed measures for each of 8 bars; dice rolls determine the sequence
- **Haydn's algorithm: the classical era's generative music system**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, IMSLP, academic sources

### Master of Orion (1993) — Simtex
- 4X space strategy with AI alien races
- Each AI race has distinct behaviors, technologies, and diplomatic approaches
- **The AI as alien diplomat: space strategy with intelligent opponents**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, GOG

### Stellaris (2016) — Paradox Development Studio
- Grand strategy space game with complex AI empires
- AI empires have distinct ethics, governments, and diplomatic behaviors
- **The AI as space emperor: grand strategy with intelligent opponents**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, Paradox

### System Shock (1994) — Looking Glass Studios
- SHODAN: a malevolent AI that takes over a space station
- One of the first games to feature a truly terrifying AI antagonist
- **SHODAN: the first terrifying AI villain in gaming**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, Night Dive Studios

### Claude Shannon: Chess-Playing Computer (1950)
- Erster theoretischer Entwurf für einen schachspielenden Computer
- Programmierung auf dem Papier, nicht ausgeführt
- **Die Vision des Computers als Spieler**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, IEEE

### Stellaris (2016) — Paradox Interactive
- AI-controlled empires with personalities, ethics, and diplomatic strategies
- The "Crisis" factions — endgame AI threats that emerge from the galaxy itself
- **AI as geopolitical actor: machine empires that negotiate and betray**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, Paradox Interactive

### Deep Blue (1997) — IBM
- First computer to defeat a reigning world chess champion (Garry Kasparov)
- Brute-force search + evaluation functions — not "learning" but calculation
- **The victory of calculation over intuition: the machine that out-calculated humanity**
- Quelle: Wikipedia, IBM, Facebook, The Refined Geek

### AlphaZero (2017) — DeepMind
- Self-play learning: taught itself chess, shogi, and Go from scratch in 24 hours
- No human games used as training data — pure self-play reinforcement learning
- **The AI that became its own teacher: the loop of self-improvement**
- Quelle: Science, DeepMind, Wikipedia
