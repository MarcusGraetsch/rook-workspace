)  will take place in Brussels this weekend. Talks on topics related to open source and digital infrastructures will be held in more than 30 themed rooms.

Our alliance will be represented on 31. 1. 2026 in the

Social Web Room

.  Cofounder

Sandra Barthel

will give a talk :

“The Social Web and Digital Sovereignty: Building Social Advocacy Networks in and for Europe.”

https://fosdem.org/2026/schedule/event/RCV98B-the-social-web-and-digital-sovereignty

Why is the alliance necessary, what are our demands, and why do we need to broaden the debate on digital sovereignty?

In the same room, there will also be further talks by alliance organisations on the topics of Fediverse and the social web.

Hannah Aubry

from Mastodon will talk about community building:

“Tending the Herd: Community at Mastodon“

https://fosdem.org/2026/schedule/event/HJYRFF-tending-the-herd

Björn Staschen

of Save Social will talk about:

“Reaching out to the wider society: Why the open social web needs to matter to more people – and how we can achieve that goal”

https://fosdem.org/2026/schedule/event/9MTT3F-reaching_out/

Our alliance friends at

Fedivariety

discuss their research project

“Fediverse Integration into (EU) Public Administration – A Fantastic FediVariety Circus”

https://fosdem.org/2026/schedule/event/PGB9VZ-a-fantastic-fedivariety-circus/

Tobias

from FSFE presents developments at Friendica in his talk

“Friendica – Hidden in plain sight since 2025”

https://fosdem.org/2026/schedule/event/DNS7Z8-friendica_-_hidden_in_plain_sight_since_2025/

On Sunday from 11:50 to 12:20, there will also be a panel discussion:

“The Fediverse and the EU’s Digital Services Act: solving the challenges of modern social media?“

https://fosdem.org/2026/schedule/event/W8RCMT-the_fediverse_and_the_eus_digital_services_act_solving_the_challenges_of_modern_/
