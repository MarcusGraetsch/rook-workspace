5 ChatGPT prompts to keep going when it’s tough (don’t give up)

getty

No entrepreneurial journey is smooth sailing. Even if the general trajectory is up, there will be bumps in the road, tough calls to make, and times when you want to curl up into a ball. And that’s okay. Even the most successful business owners had tough times.

Instead of trying to avoid tough times, develop the strength to get through them. Then keep going.

I asked entrepreneurs and business leaders how they keep going when it’s tough and

they told me in their hundreds

. Here are the top six suggestions with accompanying prompts, to use in ChatGPT, Claude, or your LLM of choice.

Stay in the game: ChatGPT prompts to progress through challenges

Get perspective

Some people have real problems. In comparison to everything that could be happening, yours are probably small. Eco-entrepreneur

Lorraine Allman

gets perspective straight off the bat. “I remind myself that if things were easy then everyone would be doing what I’m trying to do,” she said.

Social media marketer

AJ Cartas

projects his problem into the future to play it down today. “When it comes to stress, I try to think if this is gonna matter one year from now. If yes, I try to think if it’ll matter one hundred years from now.”

Emily Ross

, EMEA director of brand strategy and creative at X, added, “pressure makes diamonds.” Try this prompt to get perspective.

Do a stock take


No-code and automation entrepreneur

Nix Good

said she does “regular internal ‘stock takes’ as I call them.” She checks, “Am I taking enough screen breaks? Drinking enough water? Getting enough rest? Avoiding too much alcohol during the working week?” and makes changes accordingly. Journalist

Ruchi Kumar

has a different approach. “I start by listing all my achievements and blessings,” she said. “Everything I have done, overcome or achieved that I couldn’t have imagined five or ten years ago.” Use this prompt for your personal stock take.

“Guide me through a comprehensive 'stock take' of my current situation. List a series of questions that check my physical health, mental state, workload and current stressors, so I can identify what might be causing me to feel like times are tough. Ask me to consider the questions and suggest where the problem might lie, then suggest 3 things I can do to regain my sense of wellbeing and balance.”

I'm blessed, and I need to shut up, and get to work!” Why cry when you can laugh? Laughing makes light of what is probably not that bad.
