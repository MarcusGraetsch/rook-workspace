Hosting the ‘Engagement Lab’ on public involvement in AI governance

by

Data Justice Lab

|

Jun 24, 2025

On 24 June, the Data Justice Lab hosted a 1-day event at Cardiff University to explore opportunities and challenges for involving the public in debates and decisions on the deployment of data systems and AI. The

Engagement Lab – Embedding public voice in the local governance of data and AI innovation

brought together local government officials from England and Wales, as well as experts from civil society organisations and think tanks, for an open exchange on how to advance participation and accountability in AI governance.

With local government being at the front-line in piloting and deploying new AI and data-driven tools in the search for savings and service improvement, participants asked: How can and should local government engage with local residents when planning, evaluating and governing data and AI-enabled reforms? And what are the challenges and opportunities of involving the public in decision-making on data and AI uses?

Key issues that were discussed included: Methods for citizen engagement; getting buy-in for participatory AI projects; participatory procurement and oversight; principles and charters for citizen-oriented data/AI governance; involving those who are most affected; etc.

Co-organised with

Connected by Data

and the

Liverpool Civic Data Cooperative

, the event was part of our two current projects

Public Engagement with Public Sector AI (PEPSAI)

and

Transforming data & AI deployment by local authorities through public engagement

.
