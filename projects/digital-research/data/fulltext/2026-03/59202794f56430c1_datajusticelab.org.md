New public sector guidebook on participatory AI governance

by

Data Justice Lab

|

Dec 15, 2025

While the roll-out of AI in the public sector is accelerating, citizens and residents have little say in how public services are being datafied and automated. The new guidebook

Involving the public in AI governance

provides a useful overview for public sector leaders and officers on how to engage the public in debates and decisions about deploying AI. Written by Arne Hintz and Harry Warne, it offers examples, discusses best practices and challenges, and links to helpful resources by other organisations.

The guidebook was produced in the context of two recent Lab projects: ‘Public Engagement with Public Sector AI’ (a collaboration by Lab Co-Directors Joanna Redden and Arne Hintz) and ‘Transforming data & AI deployment by local authorities through public engagement’ (led by Arne Hintz). It was also informed by the

Engagement Lab

which we hosted at Cardiff University on 24 June 2025 to explore opportunities and challenges for involving the public in the use of data and AI systems in public services, and which brought together local government officials and experts from civil society and think tanks.
