In issuing some fairly basic and standard advice, it noted that those in telecommunications specifically should make sure their user and networking devices are kept up to date to prevent compromises to their own and their organization's security posture.

It also recommended that data

backups

should be conducted on a regular basis and that where possible, online devices should be disconnected from your network.

Router protection

When it comes to

malware

, the NSA recommended rebooting your devices - including your routers - regularly, as this can help to "remove implants". This is effective for dealing with less persistent forms of malware, and it recommends you do this at least once a week.

Turning on automatic updates was also recommended by the agency, as well as using an account on your work device that doesn't grant you privileged access to the machine.
