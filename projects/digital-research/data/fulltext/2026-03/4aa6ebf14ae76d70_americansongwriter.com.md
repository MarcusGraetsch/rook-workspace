We Asked A.I. to Rewrite the “Sympathy for the Devil” by the Rolling Stones – Take a Look at the Result

By

Alex Hopper

Updated:

May 29, 2023 9:08 am

The applications for Artificial Intelligence are quickly expanding. For the first time, the music industry is starting to really reckon with the possible effects of AI on a mass scale. What happens to the craft of songwriting when it gets boiled down to typing a few keywords into a search engine and seeing what a robot comes up with?

Videos by American Songwriter

With that future becoming a very real possibility, we decided to put AI to the test to see if it was really any match for some of the greatest minds in music.

We prompted

ChatGPT

to rewrite “Sympathy for the Devil” by the Rolling Stones. The song is generally considered one of the best rock efforts of all time. Does the AI version stack up to the original? Find out, below.

Allow Me To Introduce Myself…

This song, credited to the Jagger/Richards songwriting partnership, blames the devil for evil acts happening around the world. From the crucifixion of Jesus Christ to the Russian Revolution, Jagger’s devil character assumes responsibility.

When AI was tasked with re-writing “Sympathy for the Devil” they mirrored the original song’s rhyme scheme and sentiment using different words.

Please allow me to introduce myself

I’m a figure of complexity and grace

I’ve witnessed centuries of human toil and strife

And I’ve been there in every time and place

And watched the ebb and flow of human greed

I’ve witnessed wars that tore your world apart

And observed the choices that make your hearts bleed

Where the rewritten version diverges from the original is the addition of a traditional chorus that begs humanity to have empathy for the nefarious force. The technology’s devil character asks the human race to assume culpability.

We’re not solely to blame

, it wrote.

So won’t you please have empathy for our kind?

Understand the shadows that we bear

We’ve played our role throughout history’s pages

But we’re not solely to blame, I swear

AI innately has a creepy quality to it. Anything that mimics human ability feels a little uncanny and uncomfortable to us.


We have to give it to ChatGPT, it did come up with a pretty good song – chock full of visuals and hard questions – but, without the Stones’ original version, the technology wouldn’t be able to pull enough information to mimic Jagger and Richard’s incomparable songwriting voice.

When we prompted it to write an original song with the same premise as “Sympathy for the Devil,” it came up with this stunner of a line:

There once was a devil, oh so absurd with horns on his head and a tail that twirled

…so we think Jagger and Richards can sleep peacefully for now.
