Generation-X,

born between 1965 and 1980

, find themselves in a challenging  position. Now in their 40s and 50s, and some pushing 60, they are supposed to be at the peak of their careers, calling the shots and cashing in on decades of experience.

But for many of this cohort, it doesn’t feel that way.

Ageism is creeping in

. Job opportunities seem scarce. There is intense competition from all sides, including both younger generations, Millennials, and Boomers who aren’t leaving the workforce.

At this stage, Gen X should be hitting their stride. There are expectations that they’ve achieved lofty career goals. But not everyone is thriving. However, Gen-X is known for being tough and gritty. They were the first latchkey kids who grew up adaptable to constant change. They bridged the gap of the analog world to the digital and internet revolution. Despite their resiliency, many feel stuck in place. Others have climbed the ladder successfully.

Gen-Xers are not the “slackers” of the ‘90s anymore. They’ve earned

wisdom, grit, and a knack for bridging the old and new

. Their careers aren’t over just yet. For many, they’re just getting  started.

Success Stories

Gen-Xers are a resilient generation, known for their work ethic, independence, and indifference to what people think of them. There are many super successful Gen-Xers, despite their label of being slackers. This group boasts a large number of accomplished entrepreneurs such as Jeff Bezos, Elon Musk, Larry Page, Sergey Brin, Jack Dorsey and Mark Cuban, among many others.

Career Blocks

Despite the huge wins, many are

facing a career plateau, or worse, a freefall

. Layoffs in tech, media, and other sectors have hit this generation hard. Take creative fields, for instance. A photographer or journalist in their 50s might find their skills labeled obsolete as TikTok campaigns replace glossy spreads. Even in corporate roles, the push for digital natives has left seasoned Gen Xers scrambling to prove that they are not dinosaurs. Although there are challenges,Gen-Xers are

not coasting into retirement

. Many can’t afford to.

The Silent Career Killer

Ageism is

hitting Gen X with a vengeance

. Employers often assume that this group is set in their ways. Similar to how people view Baby Boomers. Management sometimes believe that they can’t keep up with the latest tech advancements.



Job Availability: A Shrinking Pie

The workforce is aging. Over a third of U.S. workers are 50-plus, but the roles they are suited for aren’t growing to match. Boomers, clinging to senior positions past traditional retirement age, block the pipeline. This may be one of the reasons why there are so many entrepreneurial Gen-Xers, as they needed to step outside the traditional climbing the corporate ladder.

Economic uncertainty doesn’t help. Post-pandemic layoffs, AI and automation have slashed openings. Also, this group is stuck in

the “sandwich” phase—supporting kids paying for college, and taking care of aging parents

, leaving little wiggle room to retrain or relocate.
