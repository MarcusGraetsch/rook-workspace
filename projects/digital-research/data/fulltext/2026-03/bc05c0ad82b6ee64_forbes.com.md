AI Will Change Your Child’s Life —But Not In The Way You Think

getty

I've witnessed the transformative power of Artificial Intelligence.

When working with schools on AI integration, I sometimes get asked to lead a workshop with students. In recent sessions with high school students in California, New York and the UK, they have trained AI to create a support team of virtual assistants for themselves. From virtual teachers and sleep coaches to career advisors, study partners and holiday planners, the possibilities these students have explored are both inspiring and revealing.

When given the tools to customize AI, students have naturally begun to create their own support networks of virtual bots. This shift is more than just technological. There is a leap towards developing essential entrepreneurial skills that will serve them throughout their lives.

These skills include evaluating results, managing teams, effective communication, decision-making, creative thinking, questioning and taking decisive action based on responses.

Entrepreneurial Skills in Action

Creating and using these AI assistants requires entrepreneurial abilities. Students must evaluate the effectiveness of their AI assistants. They need to be continually refined based on feedback and performance data. This evaluative process sharpens their analytical skills.

Decision-making is another critical skill that students develop. Choosing which AI assistants to create, determining their functionalities and making improvements based on results are all decisions that require thoughtful consideration. This decision-making process is enhanced by creative thinking. Students must innovate and find unique solutions to the challenges they encounter.

Questioning and taking action are the final pieces of the puzzle. Encouraging students to ask the right questions and seek out new information fosters a culture of continuous learning. Acting on the answers they find is the key to making meaningful changes and improvements.


Our current education systems too often focus on memorization and the acquisition of vast amounts of knowledge. Success does not depend on knowing everything. Leaders know this. In large part, it hinges on the ability to effectively utilize a team—be it human or virtual.

Sam Altman, CEO of OpenAI, predicted that we will soon see the first one-person $1 billion business. This single person will will be able to achieve this goal because they will have a team of AI working with them. This prediction underscores the immense potential of AI to amplify individual capabilities and achieve unprecedented success.


By shifting our focus in education towards developing entrepreneurial skills, we can better prepare students for their lives. This approach not only aligns with the realities of the modern workplace but could also fosters a more engaging and dynamic learning environment.
