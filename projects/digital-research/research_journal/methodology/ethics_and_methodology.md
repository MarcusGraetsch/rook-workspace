## 1. EPISTEMOLOGICAL FOUNDATIONS

### 1.1 The Problem of Nachvollziehbarkeit in Digital Research

Max Weber argued that social science must be *nachvollziehbar* — traceable, accountable, subject to critical examination. In the digital age, this faces new challenges:

- **LLM outputs** appear authoritative but may contain hallucinations
- **Digital sources** can disappear or change without notice
- **Algorithmic curation** shapes what sources are visible
- **Speed of research** may compromise reflection depth

This project commits to maintaining Nachvollziehbarkeit through systematic documentation.

### 1.2 Research as Construction (Konstruktionscharakter der Forschung)

Following Weber's concept of *Idealtypen* (ideal types), this research acknowledges:

- "Digital capitalism" is a heuristic construct, not a natural kind
- Theoretical frameworks organize empirical material but are themselves provisional
- Research questions shape findings; transparency about this circularity is essential

## 2. METHODOLOGICAL PRINCIPLES

### 2.1 Source Criticism (Quellenkritik)

**19th-century standard applied to 21st-century research:**

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Verification** | All sources checked for existence (web_search, web_fetch, library catalogs) |
| **Provenance** | Bibliographic details complete (author, year, publisher, ISBN/DOI) |
| **Context** | Sources located in intellectual traditions, not treated atomistically |
| **Corrections** | Errors acknowledged and documented (see Session 1: removal of "Shan") |

**Digital-specific adaptations:**
- URLs archived where possible
- PDFs stored locally when legally permissible
- Screenshots of dynamic content
- Date of access recorded

### 2.2 Transparency of Process (Verfahrenstransparenz)

**What is documented:**

1. **Session logs** (`research_journal/sessions/`)
   - Date, duration, participants
   - Actions undertaken
   - Decisions made with rationale
   - Problems encountered and solutions

2. **Decision logs** (`research_journal/decisions/`)
   - Major theoretical choices
   - Scope limitations
   - Methodological innovations

3. **Git version control**
   - Every change committed with descriptive messages
   - Full history recoverable
   - Branches for experimental directions

### 2.3 Reflexivity (Reflexivität)

**Researcher positionality:**
- Marcus: Principal investigator, German context
- Rook: AI assistant, trained on specific corpora with known limitations

**Acknowledged limitations:**
- Language constraints: German/English/limited other languages
- Access constraints: Open-access sources prioritized
- Temporal constraints: Research conducted 2026; sources may become outdated

## 3. ETHICAL COMMITMENTS

### 3.1 Intellectual Honesty (Wahrhaftigkeit)

- No fabricated sources
- No misrepresented findings
- Clear distinction between:
  - Established facts
  - Working hypotheses
  - Speculative extensions

### 3.2 Non-Extractive Research

**Engaging Global South scholarship:**
- Cite authors, not just "regions"
- Avoid tokenism (representation without engagement)
- Prioritize access to sources from underrepresented contexts

**When citing non-Western scholars:**
- Full bibliographic information
- Contextualize their intellectual traditions
- Avoid "discovery" framing

### 3.3 Accountability

**To future readers:**
- This journal enables verification of claims
- Methodological choices explained, not assumed
- Limitations acknowledged

**To research subjects:**
- Platform workers, if interviewed: informed consent, anonymization
- Living scholars cited: accurate representation

## 4. LITERATURE MANAGEMENT

### 4.1 Acquisition Standards

**Priority order:**
1. Open-access scholarly sources (DOAJ, Sci-Hub alternatives, institutional repositories)
2. Library access (university subscriptions)
3. Purchase (for essential works not otherwise available)
4. Interlibrary loan

**Prohibited:**
- Purchasing from predatory publishers
- Citing sources not personally reviewed

### 4.2 Note-Taking Protocol

**Template:** See `notes/templates/reading_note.md`

**Required elements:**
- Full citation
- 4-criteria evaluation (Epistemic, Empirical, Political, Synthetic)
- Core argument (author's words where possible)
- Critical assessment (our evaluation)
- Relation to project

**Prohibited:**
- Copy-paste without quotation marks
- Paraphrase without citation
- AI summary without verification

### 4.3 Synthesis Standards

**When combining sources:**
- Clear attribution of ideas to original authors
- Explicit marking of our interpretive additions
- Distinguish:
  - "Fuchs argues..." (author's position)
  - "We interpret Fuchs as..." (our reading)
  - "Building on Fuchs..." (our extension)

## 5. COLLABORATION PROTOCOL

### 5.1 Human-AI Collaboration: A New Methodological Category

This project operates with a **Principal Investigator (PI)** — Marcus — and an **AI Research Assistant** — Rook. This is not a traditional human-human collaboration, nor is it simply "using a tool."

**For detailed analysis, see:** [human_ai_collaboration.md](human_ai_collaboration.md)

**Key principles:**

| Role | Responsibilities | Authority |
|------|------------------|-----------|
| **Marcus (PI)** | Research direction, theoretical judgments, ethical oversight, final decisions | Can override any suggestion; owns intellectual property; accountable for outputs |
| **Rook (AI)** | Literature organization, draft writing, source verification, technical tasks | Can suggest, cannot decide; drafts require PI approval; no legal standing |

**Why this matters:**
- AI can generate "original" text but cannot take responsibility
- Human must verify but benefits from AI's pattern-matching
- Transparency about this collaboration IS a methodological contribution
- See detailed discussion in linked document

### 5.2 Transparency about AI Assistance

**Explicit marking:**
- Drafts generated by AI marked as such
- Session logs distinguish PI and AI contributions
- Errors (like hallucinated sources) traced to AI, corrected by PI
- Final responsibility remains with human researcher (Marcus)

### 5.3 Documentation of Collaboration

**Recorded in:**
- Session logs (who did what, who decided)
- Git commits (what changed)
- This methodology statement
- [human_ai_collaboration.md](human_ai_collaboration.md) (detailed analysis)
